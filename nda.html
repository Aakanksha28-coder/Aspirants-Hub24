National Defence Academy

The National Defence Academy (NDA) is a preeminent institution in India, serving as the breeding ground for the country's future military leaders. Nestled amidst the scenic landscapes of Khadakwasla, Pune, the NDA holds a distinguished legacy dating back to its establishment in 1954. As a tri-service academy, it plays a pivotal role in shaping the officers for the Indian Army, Navy, and Air Force.

The NDA offers a comprehensive training program that encompasses academics, physical fitness, and character development. Cadets undergo a rigorous curriculum that includes military sciences, leadership training, and outdoor activities. The academy's emphasis on discipline and the 'code of conduct' fosters a sense of responsibility and integrity among the cadets.

The hallmark of NDA lies not only in its academic and military training but also in the holistic approach to developing well-rounded individuals. The lush green campus provides an ideal environment for imbibing leadership skills and instilling a spirit of camaraderie among the cadets.

Graduates from the NDA go on to become officers in various branches of the armed forces, embodying the institution's motto - "Service before Self." The academy stands as a testament to India's commitment to producing leaders who are not only academically proficient but also possess the character and fortitude to face the diverse challenges of defending the nation. NDA remains a beacon of excellence, contributing significantly to the nation's defense and security apparatus.

ABOUT EXAM

National Defence Academy Entrance Exam(NDA):

The NDA (National Defence Academy) exam is a highly prestigious and competitive examination in India that serves as a gateway for aspiring candidates to join the Indian Army, Navy, and Air Force. Conducted twice a year by the Union Public Service Commission (UPSC), the NDA exam is designed to select and train candidates for a career in defense services.

SELECTION PROCESS: The Indian Armed Forces selects candidates for NDA in three stages
STAGE 1: NDA Written Examination is conducted twice every year. NDA 1 is conducted in the month of April and for NDA 2 in September.
STAGE 2 : Candidates qualifying written exam will be called to appear for SSB Interviews at Armed Forces SSB centers. Stage 2 will include:
- SSB Interviews
- Document Verification
- Medical Examination
STAGE 3 : After clearing stage 2 merit list of selected candidates will be released. Merit list will be based on marks obtained in stage 1 and 2.

Selected candidates : Selected candidates will get their joining letters and will have to report at NDA for Training, after completion of which they will join their respective service academies.

Training at NDA: Once selected, candidates undergo a rigorous three-year training program at the NDA, where they receive academic, military, and physical training to groom them into future leaders of the armed forces.

Career Opportunities:After successful completion of training, candidates are commissioned as officers in the Indian Army, Navy, or Air Force, depending on their preferences and merit.

Prestige and Honor: Serving in the armed forces is considered a matter of great pride and honor in India. The NDA exam, therefore, attracts a pool of talented and dedicated youth who aspire to contribute to the nation's defense and security.

Preparing for the NDA exam requires a comprehensive approach, covering not only academic subjects but also physical fitness and personality development. It is a challenging yet rewarding journey for those who dream of a career in the defense services.




ELIGIBILITY CRITERIA

The eligibility criteria for the National Defence Academy (NDA) exam, conducted by the Union Public Service Commission (UPSC) in India, are as follows:

Nationality:
A candidate must be a citizen of India, or
A subject of Bhutan, or
A subject of Nepal, or
A Tibetan refugee who came to India before January 1, 1962, with the intention of permanently settling in India, or
A person of Indian origin who has migrated from Pakistan, Burma, Sri Lanka, and East African Countries of Kenya, Uganda, the United Republic of Tanzania, Zambia, Malawi, Zaire, and Ethiopia or Vietnam with the intention of permanently settling in India.

Age Limit:
For NDA-I: Candidates born not earlier than July 2, 2003, and not later than January 1, 2007, are eligible.
For NDA-II: Candidates born not earlier than January 2, 2004, and not later than July 1, 2007, are eligible.
The age limit is calculated based on the date of birth mentioned in the Matriculation/Secondary School Examination certificate.

Educational Qualifications:
For Army Wing of NDA: 10+2 pattern of School Education or equivalent examination conducted by a recognized board.
For Air Force and Naval Wings of NDA and for the 10+2 Cadet Entry Scheme at the Indian Naval Academy: 10+2 pattern of School Education or equivalent with Physics and Mathematics conducted by a recognized board.

Physical Fitness:
Candidates must be physically fit as per the standards set by the National Defence Academy and Naval Academy Examination.

Marital Status:
Candidates must be unmarried. Marriage is not permitted during training. Candidates who marry during training are liable to be discharged and will be required to refund all expenditure incurred on them by the government.

Gender: The NDA exam is for both male and female candidates.

It's important for candidates to carefully review and fulfill all the eligibility criteria before applying for the NDA exam. The UPSC may introduce changes to the eligibility criteria, so candidates are advised to refer to the official UPSC notification and guidelines for the most accurate and up-to-date information.




EXAM PATTERN

The NDA exam is known for its rigorous selection process, evaluating not only the academic prowess of candidates but also their leadership qualities, communication skills, and overall personality. It is a highly competitive and esteemed examination for those aspiring to serve in the defense forces of India.Here is an overview of the examination process:

The NDA Exam Pattern is divided into two stages:
1.Written Examination
2.SSB Interview (Service Selection Board)


WRITTEN EXAMINATION
Mode of Exam : Offline
The Maths and GAT papers make up the NDA written test. The GAT will be conducted for 600 marks, while the Maths exam is for 300 marks. Both papers have a 150-minute/2.5-hour time limit.

The written exam is further divided into the following:
1] Mathematics
2] GAT (General Ability Test)
The Mathematics marks is about 300 and the GAT is at 600 marks.

Negative Marking Scheme is as follows :
-For Mathematics, 2.5 for each correct answer and 0.83 deducted for each incorrect answer.
-For GAT, 4 for each correct answer and 1.33 deducted for each incorrect answer.

Result:Candidates who clear the written examination move on to the next stage.(i.e SSB)


SERVICES SELECTION BOARD (SSB) INTERVIEW
1] SSB Interview Process:
Candidates who qualify in the written exam undergo an interview conducted by the Services Selection Board. The SSB interview comprises two stages: Stage I and Stage II.

Stage I:
-Intelligence and Personality Test
-Officer Potentiality Test (OPT)

Stage II:
-Interview
-Group Testing Officer Tasks
-Psychology Tests
-Conference

2]Medical Examination:
Candidates who clear the SSB interview undergo a thorough medical examination.

3]Final Merit List:
The final merit list is prepared based on the candidate's performance in the written examination and the SSB interview.




SYLLABUS

It's essential for candidates to thoroughly study and understand the concepts covered in the NDA exam syllabus.Here's a detailed breakdown of the NDA exam syllabus:

A] Mathematics:
a) Algebra :
- Concepts of sets, operations on sets, complex numbers, quadratic equations, and linear equations.
b) Matrices and Determinants :
- Types of matrices, operations on matrices, determinants of a matrix, and solutions of linear equations using matrices.
c) Trigonometry :
- Angles and their measures, trigonometric identities, sine, cosine, and tangent of angles, trigonometric equations.
d) Analytical Geometry :
- Cartesian coordinate system, straight lines, circles, conic sections, three-dimensional geometry.
e) Differential Calculus :
- Concept of a function, limit, continuity, derivatives, and their applications.
f) Integral Calculus and Differential Equations :
- Integration and its applications, differential equations.
g) Vector Algebra :
- Vectors and scalars, vector operations, and their applications.
h) Statistics and Probability :
- Classification of data, frequency distribution, cumulative frequency, mean, median, mode, variance, standard deviation, and probability.


B] General Ability Test (GAT):
a) English :
- Grammar and usage, vocabulary, comprehension, and cohesion in extended text.
b) General Knowledge :
- Physics, Chemistry, General Science, Social Studies, Geography, Current Events, and topics related to the freedom movement, etc.
c) Physics :
- Physical properties and states of matter, mass, weight, volume, density, and force, motion, and energy.
d) Chemistry :
- Physical and chemical changes, elements, mixtures, and compounds, acids, bases, and salts.
e) General Science :
- Basic concepts of science, the scientific method, and its applications.
f) History, Freedom Movement, etc. :
- Significant events and figures related to the history of India.
g) Geography :
- Earth's atmosphere and its composition, physical geography, and important geographical features of India.
h) Current Events :
- Matters of everyday observation and experience, as well as questions related to India and its neighboring countries.
