Combined Defence Services Examination

The Combined Defence Services (CDS) examination stands as a gateway for countless aspirants in India, offering them a passage into the esteemed realms of the Indian Army, Navy, and Air Force. Administered by the Union Public Service Commission (UPSC).The journey of aspiring defenders through the CDS examination is more than a career choice; it is a calling, a commitment to serve and protect the nation. It's a journey that tests not just academic proficiency but also character, resilience, and a sense of duty. Beyond the uniform and the prestige, it's a dedication to a life of honor, courage, and sacrifice.

The written examination, the first hurdle, is a formidable test of knowledge and aptitude.This written phase is a testament to the aspirant's ability to balance intellectual acumen with practical skills, mirroring the demands of a military career.Following success in the written examination, candidates enter the arduous phase of the interview conducted by the Service Selection Board (SSB). Post the interview, the journey continues with a thorough medical examination

Successful candidates are then inducted into various prestigious training academies, each with its unique rigors and challenges. The Indian Military Academy (IMA), situated in Dehradun, shapes officers for the Indian Army. The Indian Naval Academy (INA) in Ezhimala, Kerala, prepares individuals for a life at sea, instilling naval traditions and expertise. The Air Force Academy (AFA) in Hyderabad molds aspiring aviators, imparting knowledge and skills crucial for aerial warfare. The Officers' Training Academy (OTA), with its locations in Chennai and Gaya, trains officers for the Indian Army, emphasizing leadership and professional development.

ABOUT EXAM

Combined Defence Services(CDS) Examination :

Indian Armed Forces are one of the best armed forces in the world. It commands an arsenal of modern weaponry, advanced communication systems, but its real strength lies in the men who keep the Indian flag flying high. UPSC conducts CDS Exam twice every year to induct Officers the Indian Armed Forces. This is an excellent opportunity for candidates who wish to join the Indian Armed Forces and serve the nation.

SELECTION PROCESS: The Indian Armed Forces selects candidates in CDS in three stages
STAGE 1: CDS Written Examination is conducted twice every year. CDS 1 is conducted in the month of April and for CDS 2 in September.
STAGE 2 : Candidates qualifying written exam will be called to appear for SSB Interviews at Armed Forces SSB centers. Stage 2 will include:
- SSB Interviews
- Document Verification
- Medical Examination
STAGE 3 : The final selection is based on the combined performance in the written examination and the SSB interview.


Selected candidates : Selected candidates join different training academies based on their preferences and the type of commission they are eligible for:
- Indian Military Academy (IMA) for Army.
- Indian Naval Academy (INA) for Navy.
- Air Force Academy (AFA) for Air Force.
- Officers' Training Academy (OTA) for Short Service Commission in the Army.

Training: After successfully clearing the Combined Defence Services (CDS) examination, candidates undergo training at various prestigious military academies. The training is designed to impart the necessary skills, knowledge, and discipline required for a career in the armed forces. The training duration and focus areas depend on the academy for which the candidate has qualified.
Here's an Overview of training academies:

1] Indian Military Academy (IMA):
- Location: Dehradun, Uttarakhand.
- Training Duration: Approximately 18 months.
- Focus Areas:
i) Military training with an emphasis on leadership.
ii) Academic curriculum covering a range of subjects related to military science.
iii) Physical training to enhance fitness and endurance.

2] Indian Naval Academy (INA):
- Location: Ezhimala, Kerala.
- Training Duration: Four years, including a three-year degree course and one year of naval training.
- Focus Areas:
i) Academic training with a focus on naval subjects for the first three years.
ii) Professional naval training in the final year, including sea training and practical experience.

3] Air Force Academy (AFA):
Location: Dundigal, Hyderabad.
Training Duration: Approximately 74 weeks.
Focus Areas:
i) Flying and technical training for flying branches.
ii) Ground duty officers undergo specialized training in their respective fields.
iii) Physical fitness and leadership training.

4] Officers' Training Academy (OTA):
- Location: Chennai, Tamil Nadu (for men), and Gaya, Bihar (for women).
- Training Duration:
For IMA and OTA (Men): Approximately 49 weeks.
For OTA (Women): Approximately 11 months.
- Focus Areas:
i) Leadership training for short service commissioned officers.
ii) Academic curriculum covering military subjects.
iii) Physical training and character development.

Prestige and Honor: Passing the CDS exam is not just an academic accomplishment; it signifies a commitment to a life of service, leadership, and sacrifice. The prestige associated with being a CDS officer extends beyond personal achievement to a recognition of one's role in safeguarding the nation's integrity and security.




ELIGIBILITY CRITERIA

The eligibility criteria for the National Defence Academy (NDA) exam, conducted by the Union Public Service Commission (UPSC) in India, are as follows:

Nationality:
A candidate must be a citizen of India, or
A subject of Bhutan, or
A subject of Nepal, or
A Tibetan refugee who came to India before January 1, 1962, with the intention of permanently settling in India, or
A person of Indian origin who has migrated from Pakistan, Burma, Sri Lanka, and East African Countries of Kenya, Uganda, the United Republic of Tanzania, Zambia, Malawi, Zaire, and Ethiopia or Vietnam with the intention of permanently settling in India.

Age Limit:
For IMA, INA, AFA unmarried male candidates of age 19 to 24 years.
For OTA unmarried male and female candidates of age 20 to 25 years.
The age limit is calculated based on the date of birth mentioned in the Matriculation/Secondary School Examination certificate.

Educational Qualifications:
For IMA and OTA candidate must have a graduate Degree in any discipline from a recognized university.
For INA, candidates must have completed graduation in Engineering from a recognized university.
For AFA candidates must have degree of a recognized university (with Physics and Mathematics at 10+2) or a Bachelor’s degree in Engineering.

Physical Fitness:
According to the CDS eligibility, candidates must meet the prescribed physical standards for fitness.
- They must be in good physical and mental health and free from any disease/disability which can interfere with the performance of duties.
- There should be no evidence of weak constitution, bodily defects, or underweight. The candidate should not be overweight or obese.
- Candidates should have no past history of mental breakdown or fits.
- The minimum CDS height required for male candidates is 157.5 cms (157 cms for the Navy and 162.5 cms for Air Force).
For women candidates, the minimum CDS height required is 152 cm. However, the minimum height for all entries is 148 cm after all types of waivers/dispensations.
For Male Candidates, the chest should be well developed.
- The Body Mass Index of the candidates should be below 25.

Marital Status:
For MALE,
i) IMA & INA - Unmarried
ii) IAFA - Candidates who are below the age limit of 25 years must be unmarried. Candidates won’t be allowed to get married during the training period.As the IAFA provides age relaxation for certain candidates, Candidates who are above the age of 25 years and are married can apply but during training, no accommodation for a spouse would be provided nor will the candidates be allowed to live with family outside the premises.
iii) OTA - Unmarried

For FEMALE,
OTA - Unmarried females, Widows who have not remarried, and Divorcees who have not remarried and possess divorce documents are eligible.

Gender: Both unmarried males and females are eligible for the CDS exam.
whereas, Female candidates are only eligible for OTA .

It's important for candidates to carefully review and fulfill all the eligibility criteria before applying for the CDS exam. The UPSC may introduce changes to the eligibility criteria, so candidates are advised to refer to the official UPSC notification and guidelines for the most accurate and up-to-date information.




EXAM PATTERN

The exam pattern varies depending on the academy for which the candidate is applying. The academies include the Indian Military Academy (IMA), Indian Naval Academy (INA), Air Force Academy (AFA), and Officers' Training Academy (OTA). Here's the exam pattern for the CDS exam:



WRITTEN EXAMINATION : (For IMA, INA, and AFA)
Mode of Exam : Offline

Paper I: English (100 marks)
Paper II: General Knowledge (100 marks)
Paper III: Elementary Mathematics (100 marks)
Objective type questions.
Duration: 2 hours.
Note: Papers I and II are mandatory for all candidates.Paper III is for candidates applying for the Air Force Academy.

Only Papers I and II are applicable for candidates applying for OTA.



SERVICES SELECTION BOARD (SSB) INTERVIEW
1] Stage I - Screening:
- Officer Intelligence Rating (OIR) Test: This includes verbal and non-verbal reasoning tests to assess the candidate's intelligence.
- Picture Perception and Description Test (PP&DT): Candidates are shown a picture for 30 seconds and then have to write a story based on the picture.This is followed by group discussion.

2] Stage II - Psychological Testing:
Thematic Apperception Test (TAT): Candidates are shown a series of ambiguous pictures and are required to write a story based on each picture.
Word Association Test (WAT): Candidates are shown a word for 15 seconds and have to write the first thought that comes to their mind.
Situation Reaction Test (SRT): Candidates respond to a series of situations with their spontaneous thoughts.
Self-Description Test (SD): Candidates provide a self-description based on various parameters.

3} GTO (Group Testing Officer) Tasks:
Group Discussion (GD): Candidates discuss a topic in a group.
Group Planning Exercise (GPE): Candidates solve a military-related problem as a group.
Progressive Group Task (PGT): A set of obstacles that the group must overcome together.
Half Group Task (HGT): Similar to PGT but with half the group members.
Individual Obstacles: Candidates tackle individual obstacles.
Command Task: Candidates are appointed as commanders and given a task to be completed with the help of subordinates. Lecturette: Candidates speak on a given topic for 3 minutes.

4] Interview:
Personal Interview: The candidate faces a one-on-one interview with an interviewing officer.
Conference: The final assessment where all the assessors discuss and decide on the suitability of the candidate.

5] Final Selection:
The final selection is based on the candidate's performance in all the stages of the SSB interview.A comprehensive evaluation of the candidate's personality, leadership qualities, communication skills, and overall suitability for a career in the armed forces is considered.



MEDICAL EXAMINATION
Candidates who clear the SSB interview undergo a thorough medical examination to ensure they meet the physical and medical standards required for military service.



Then,A merit list is prepared, and candidates are selected for various academies based on their preferences, performance, and the availability of vacancies.




SYLLABUS

It's essential for candidates to thoroughly study and understand the concepts covered in the CDS exam syllabus.The syllabus for the CDS examination varies for different academies,Here is a broad overview of the CDS exam syllabus:

English (Paper I):
- Grammar and usage.
- Vocabulary.
- Comprehension.
- Fill in the blanks.
- Spotting errors.
- Sentence improvement.
- Ordering of words/sentences.


2. General Knowledge (Paper II):
- Current Affairs: Events of national and international importance.
- History: Indian and World History.
- Geography: Physical, social, and economic Geography of India and the world.
- Polity: Indian Constitution, political system, and governance.
- Economics: Basics of Economics and issues related to the Indian economy.
- General Science: Physics, Chemistry, Biology, and Environmental Science.
- Defense-related topics: Defense organizations, wars, and security-related issues.


3. Elementary Mathematics (Paper III):
- Arithmetic:
i)Number system.
ii)Fundamental operations.
iii)Unitary method.
iv)Elementary number theory.
v)Remainder theorem.

- Algebra:
i)Basic operations.
ii)Simple factors.
iii)HCF and LCM.
iv)Quadratic equations.

- Trigonometry:
i)Sine, cosine, tangent.
ii)Values of sin, cos, and tan at 0, 30, 45, 60, and 90 degrees.

- Geometry:
i)Lines and angles.
ii)Triangles.
iii)Quadrilaterals.
iv)Circles.

- Mensuration:
i)Areas of squares, rectangles, parallelograms, and circles.
ii)Volumes of cubes, cuboids, spheres, and cylinders.

- Statistics and Probability:
i)Collection and tabulation of statistical data.
ii)Frequency distribution.
iii)Measures of central tendency.
iv)Probability.
