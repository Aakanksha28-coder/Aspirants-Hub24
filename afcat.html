ABOUT EXAM

Air Force Common Admission Test (AFCAT):

The Air Force Common Admission Test (AFCAT) is a competitive examination conducted by the Indian Air Force (IAF) to select officers for various branches, including Flying Branch, Ground Duty (Technical), and Ground Duty (Non-Technical). The primary goal of AFCAT is to recruit talented and motivated individuals who aspire to serve as officers in the Indian Air Force.

SELECTION PROCESS: The Indian Airforce Force selects candidates in three stages
STAGE 1: AFCAT written Examination is conducted twice in a year
STAGE 2 : Candidates who successfully clear the written examination proceed to the Services Selection Board (SSB) interview.
- Candidates who pass the SSB interview undergo a thorough medical examination.
- Document Verification

STAGE 3 : The final selection is based on a combination of the candidate's performance in the written examination and the SSB interview.

Selected candidates : The Indian Air Force prepares a merit list, and candidates who feature on this list are offered admission into various branches of the Air Force.

Training: Selected candidates undergo training at the prestigious Air Force Academy (AFA) located in Dundigal, Hyderabad. The training is designed to instill discipline, leadership, and technical skills essential for their respective branches. The duration and focus of training vary based on the branch for which the candidate is selected.

Courses Offered: AFCAT offers entry into various branches, each with its own set of courses. These include the Flying Branch, Ground Duty (Technical) Branch, and Ground Duty (Non-Technical) Branch. Successful candidates may be granted Short Service Commission (SSC) or Permanent Commission (PC) based on the branch and course.

Prestige and Honor: Successfully passing AFCAT is not just an academic achievement; it symbolizes a commitment to a life of service, leadership, and sacrifice. The prestige associated with being an officer in the Indian Air Force extends beyond personal accomplishment to a recognition of one's role in safeguarding the nation's integrity and security.

Preparing for the AFCAT exam requires a comprehensive approach, covering not only academic subjects but also physical fitness and personality development. It is a challenging yet rewarding journey for those who dream of a career in the defense services.




ELIGIBILITY CRITERIA

The eligibility criteria for the AFCAT exam, conducted by the Indian Airforce in India, are as follows:

Nationality:
A candidate must be a citizen of India, or
A subject of Bhutan, or
A subject of Nepal, or
A Tibetan refugee who came to India before January 1, 1962, with the intention of permanently settling in India, or
A person of Indian origin who has migrated from Pakistan, Burma, Sri Lanka, and East African Countries of Kenya, Uganda, the United Republic of Tanzania, Zambia, Malawi, Zaire, and Ethiopia or Vietnam with the intention of permanently settling in India.

Age Limit:
For Flying Branch (AFCAT and NCC Entry) unmarried male and female candidates of age 20 to 24 years.
For Ground Duty (Technical and Non-Technical) unmarried male and female candidates of age 20 to 26 years.
The age limit is calculated based on the date of birth mentioned in the Matriculation/Secondary School Examination certificate.

Educational Qualifications:
- Technical Branch: Candidate must have a degree from a recognized university in the field of Aeronautical / Mechanical / Electrical Engineering with a minimum of 60% marks in aggregate.
- For Flying Branch, a Graduate Degree with minimum of 60% marks in Mathematics and Physics at 10+2 level are required.
- Non-Technical Branch: Graduate Degree (min. three years degree course) in any discipline from a recognized university with a minimum of 60% marks or equivalent.

Physical Fitness:
Candidates must be physically fit as per the standards set by the Indian Airforce.

Marital Status:
Candidates must be unmarried.

Gender: The AFCAT exam is for both male and female candidates.

It's important for candidates to carefully review and fulfill all the eligibility criteria before applying for the AFCAT exam. The Air force may introduce changes to the eligibility criteria, so candidates are advised to refer to the official AFCAT notification and guidelines for the most accurate and up-to-date information.




EXAM PATTERN

The selection process for AFCAT (Air Force Common Admission Test) is a comprehensive and rigorous series of stages designed to assess the aptitude, personality, and suitability of candidates for a career as officers in the Indian Air Force. Here is a general overview of the AFCAT exam pattern:

The AFCAT Exam Pattern is divided into two stages:
1.Written Examination
2.SSB Interview (Service Selection Board)


AFCAT WRITTEN EXAMINATION:
Mode of Exam : AFCAT is a computer-based test (online mode).*on centre
The duration of the AFCAT exam is typically 2 hours (120 minutes).

The paper is objective in nature consisting of 100 questions on - Subjects
- Verbal Ability
- Numerical Ability
- General Awareness
- Reasoning and Military Aptitude

Engineering Knowledge Test (EKT) (for Technical Branch): Candidates applying for the Technical Branch need to appear for an additional Engineering Knowledge Test (EKT). The EKT is conducted immediately after the AFCAT and assesses candidates' engineering knowledge in their respective disciplines.

Negative Marking Scheme is as follows :
Each question holds 3 mark, awarded for every correct answer. There is negative marking, and 1 mark is deducted for every incorrect answer. No marks are deducted for not attempting the question.


SERVICES SELECTION BOARD (SSB) INTERVIEW
SSB Interview Process:
Candidates clearing the online examination will get their SSB Interview call up letter.
SSB Interview is 5 day long process conducted at Airforce Selection Boards (AFSB).

Day 1 - Screening and Documentation
Day 2 - Psychological tests and Interviews
Day 3 - GTO Tests and Interviews
Day 4 - PABT, CPSS and Interviews
Day 5 - Conference

Medical Examination:
Candidates clearing the SSB are called recommended candidates and stay back for Medical Examinations.
Medical Examination of recommended candidates is conducted at base hospitals.

Final Merit List:
The final selection is based on a combination of the candidate's performance in the written examination and the SSB interview.And it is released by Indian Airforce on official website. Selected candidates can download their joining letters from official Indian Airforce Website.




SYLLABUS

The AFCAT (Air Force Common Admission Test) syllabus is designed to assess candidates on various subjects, relevant to a career in the Indian Air Force. The syllabus typically covers the following areas:

A] General Awareness:
- History
- Geography
- Polity
- Current Affairs
- Environment and Basic Science
- Defense
- Art and Culture
- Sports
- Awards
- Books and Authors


B] Verbal Ability in English:
- Comprehension
- Error Detection
- Sentence Completion/Filling in of correct word
- Synonyms and Antonyms
- Testing of Vocabulary


C] Numerical Ability:
- Decimal Fraction
- Simplification
- Average
- Profit & Loss
- Percentage
- Ratio & Proportion - Time & Work
- Time & Distance


D] Reasoning and Military Aptitude Test:
- Verbal Skills
- Spatial Ability
- General Intelligence
- Puzzles, Analogy & Series
- Pattern Recognition
- Embedded Figures
- Coding and Decoding


E] Engineering Knowledge Test (EKT) (For Technical Branch):
For candidates applying for the Technical Branch, the EKT assesses their engineering knowledge in their respective disciplines.
The syllabus for EKT is based on the candidate's engineering background and covers topics related to their specific engineering discipline.
